About this blog

06 July 2016

So here I am with another blog, started in the middle of 2016. I bet the question on most minds is Why bother? Well at least it is on mine, so I'm going to answer it anyway.

Why this blog?

About four years ago I was the usual nerd you'd find flocking the front desk in every classroom around the world, with a dash of typical nerdy hobbies like reading, quizzing, coding etc. I gradually underwent a tremendous transformation from a typical nerd to a maker (more like a wannabe maker). The turning point for me was the realization that not only could I do what I was interested in for the rest of my life, but actually get good at making and make an impact through my work.

While this might seem trivial to most readers, this realization defines what I do today and is the ethos of this blog. I aim to induct more people into the maker culture and let others know about what I'm doing through it.

What is it about?

Enough about the background story already, right? This blog, as the description states, is going to be all about the science and art of making.

Now, what exactly constitutes making in this context? Absolutely everything. My posts are going to cover a wide range of topics ranging from perhaps a new paper in statistical physics to a some new electronics or science project I undertake. As evident from the the name, a lot of my posts are going to be about or use computers, but my thoughts and ideas certainly go beyond.

There is one thing I promise, though. None of my posts will be boring if you read them with the right outlook.

Why Ghost?

There are a ton of blogging platforms to choose from. Why in the world did I choose a rather cumbersome to set up platform? Here are a few reasons which might make sense

  • Markdown ♥️. Impeccable markdown support with a beautiful interface is one of the prime reasons I chose Ghost over others.
  • Ghost is beautiful. Not much to say about that.
  • Offline editing. Right now I edit the blog offline, check that everything works, and then push it to Github Pages with buster. It's so much more convenient.
  • It's convenient for code. Since a lot my content is going to involve computer science, it is inevitable that some code will creep in.
  • Markdown yet again. And I can write and edit my posts on my Kindle Paperwhite. Who even edits stuff on Kindle? I do. (I think I'll write a post on doing nice stuff on Kindle later)

Who are you anyway?

Well if you've stuck around till now without knowing this, thank you! This question, almost trivial for most people, is incredibly hard for me to answer.

I am Saarthak, an 18 year old wannabe maker. People often associate where they live or where they went to school with their identities, but honestly I don't deem those important enough to let them define me. Who am I? I am who I am, and I won't tell you because I want to let you form your own opinion.

Thanks for reading my ramblings! Here's a cat GIF for you.

S Sachdeva
